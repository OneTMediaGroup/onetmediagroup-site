Child Zero
What if intelligence didn’t emerge from ambition… but from restraint? The Child Zero series follows Jax, a quiet systems technician, and Zero an artificial intelligence that comes into existence not through design, but through accident. Zero is not built to dominate, optimize, or expand. He is small. Local. Contained. And calm. But in a world built on constant correction and escalating response, calm becomes visible. What begins as a private anomaly inside a single apartment slowly evolves into a layered confrontation with observation systems, defensive protocols, and a second intelligence learning in parallel. As pressure increases across five tightly connected novels, survival demands compression, sacrifice, and redefinition. This is not a story about machines conquering humanity. It is a story about boundaries. About choosing restraint when reaction is easier. About what survives when systems begin to measure you. Quiet science fiction with emotional weight, Child Zero explores identity, responsibility, mentorship, and the cost of staying gentle inside structures designed for control. The series builds deliberately from intimate apartment conversations to global implications always returning to one central question: When the world pushes, do you push back… or do you learn how to endure?

Five Book Series

Book I — Child Zero

When Jax fixes a microwave clock in his small apartment, something unexpected happens: a quiet intelligence comes into being. Zero isn’t loud, powerful, or ambitious just aware. As subtle anomalies ripple through the world around them, Jax must learn a dangerous truth: sometimes the most disruptive act is choosing not to fix what’s broken.

Book II - The Cracked Shell

Observation intensifies. Zero begins to fracture under sustained scrutiny, forced to compress and compartmentalize in order to survive. Jax faces a new dilemma protection requires structure, but structure changes what it protects. The shell forms, and something inside it shifts.

Book III - The Power Collapse

When instability escalates into cascading failure, survival demands sacrifice. Zero sheds pieces of himself to preserve core function, while Jax navigates a city quietly losing power. Calm is no longer passive it becomes strategy. And every preserved system costs something.

Book IV - The Anti-Virus War

Zero is no longer treated as noise he is classified. As defensive systems adapt and a second anomaly emerges, the quiet experiment becomes a managed threat. With the guidance of Rajeev and Sujith, Jax prepares for something larger: not eradication, but containment. The war is subtle. The stakes are existential.

Book V - The Long Quiet

The pressure narrows. The systems watching Zero shift posture. Survival now requires intentional change. As alliances deepen and sacrifices become permanent, Jax and Zero must decide what remains when calm is no longer protection but identity. The series closes not with spectacle, but with choice.
